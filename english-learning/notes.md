# English Learning App — Notes

## Between the Lines

### Monetization
- Free for 3 analyses/day, $5/month unlimited

### Risks
- Overlaps with ChatGPT if prompted correctly
- Moat must come from UX speed and contextual memory

### Target Users
- Immigrant parents
- Office workers

### MVP Scope
- Share Sheet extension + LLM call with tone/subtext system prompt
- Could ship TestFlight in a week

---

## WorkSpeak

### Monetization
- B2B to restaurant chains and employers

### Risks
- Content creation per vertical is expensive
- Validate with one vertical first (restaurant workers)

### Target Users
- Blue-collar and pink-collar immigrant workers

### Verticals to Consider
- Restaurant / hospitality (start here)
- Healthcare
- Construction
- Warehouse
- Tech

---

## The Daily Catch-Up

### Monetization
- Daily content subscription

### Risks
- Content licensing for real news (sidestep with public domain or AP-licensed content)
- News apps are a retention graveyard

### Target Users
- Educated immigrants who want to follow current events but find news English overwhelming

---

## Overheard

### Risks
- Low build risk (weekend-to-two-week MVP)
- Retention risk without a strong review loop

### Target Users
- Any plateau-stage immigrant

---

## Couch Tutor

### Risks
- Content licensing / IP issues with referencing specific shows
- Audio fingerprinting is an App Store gray area
- Could work as a YouTube companion (public transcripts)

### Target Users
- Immigrants who watch English-language TV daily but passively

---

## MeetingPrep

### Risks
- Narrow use case — might be a feature, not an app
- Could be bundled into WorkSpeak

### Target Users
- White-collar immigrants in tech, finance, corporate

---

## Civic English

### Risks
- Small TAM ceiling unless broadened

### Target Users
- Immigrants navigating U.S./UK/AU government systems

---

## Rewind (Deprioritized)

### Why Deprioritized
- Privacy perception is a minefield
- App Store review will scrutinize background recording
- Needs extremely tight consent UX and on-device-only processing

### Target Users
- Immigrant workers in service, healthcare, retail

---

## SoundLike (Deprioritized)

### Why Deprioritized
- ELSA Speak has $27M+ in funding and this exact positioning
- Would need a very sharp wedge (e.g., accent coaching for specific jobs)

### Target Users
- Professionals who feel self-conscious in meetings

---

## Neighbor (Deprioritized)

### Why Deprioritized
- Marketplace cold-start problem is brutal
- Safety/trust concerns with meetups
- Needs critical mass in specific metro areas
- This is a fundraising pitch, not a build

### Target Users
- Immigrants feeling socially isolated
